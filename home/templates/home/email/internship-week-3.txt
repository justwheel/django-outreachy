Outreachy week 3: Assignments for mentors & interns

Welcome to the third week of your Outreachy internship! We hope you're starting to get the hang of your internship. Please reach out to organizers@outreachy.org if you need additional help!

We have some goals for interns. Please read the sections below.

Week 3 Focus
------------

This week focuses on project clarity and communication.

As part of the new reporting structure, interns will begin documenting their work through public project reports rather than blog posts. These reports are designed to clearly explain what you are working on, how your project is structured, and how your work fits into your open source community.

Your first report helps set shared expectations between you, your mentors, your community, and the Outreachy organizers.


Week 3 Mentor Questions
-----------------------

Mentors, if you have questions or are running into challenges, please tell us.

We encourage mentors to ask questions on the private mentor chat channel. Past Outreachy mentors and coordinators often have good advice. Other mentors and coordinators may also respond faster than Outreachy organizers.

https://chat.outreachy.org/#narrow/stream/29-Mentors-.26.20Coordinators

If you have a sensitive or confidential situation, please contact Outreachy organizers directly. Outreachy organizers are available via DM on the Outreachy Zulip chat, or via email to <organizers@outreachy.org>.

Week 3 Assignment: Initial Project Report
-----------------------

Interns are required to write an Initial Project Report.

This report should be public and focus on the project itself rather than personal reflection.

Your Initial Project Report should include:

- A brief overview of your open source community and project
- What problem your project is addressing
- What you plan to work on during the internship
- Key milestones or deliverables
- Your initial project timeline
- Any known risks, dependencies, or open questions

The goal of this report is to clearly explain what you are working on and how the project will progress. This report will serve as a reference point for your mentor, your community, and Outreachy organizers when reviewing project progress.

You will revisit and update this plan in your midpoint project report later in the internship.

If you have any questions, please contact us!
